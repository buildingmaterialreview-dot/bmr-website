In-Depth Review

Build Quality & Design

The DeWalt DCF887 immediately impresses with its robust construction. The rubberized grip provides excellent control even with sweaty hands during extended use on site. After 6 months of daily professional use, there's minimal wear on the housing, which speaks volumes about DeWalt's build quality.

Weighing just 1.5kg with a 5.0Ah battery attached, it's one of the lighter impact drivers in its class. This might not sound significant, but when you're driving hundreds of screws throughout the day, that reduced weight translates to less fatigue and better control.

Performance Testing Results

We tested the DCF887 across various applications over 6 months:

  • Deck building: Drove 500+ 100mm screws into hardwood without any issues. Precision mode prevented over-driving.
  • Metal fabrication: Self-drilling screws into 3mm steel were handled with ease on speed 3.
  • Framing work: 75mm coach screws into timber posts - no problem whatsoever.
  • Delicate work: Speed 1 with precision mode perfect for cabinet installation and fixtures.

Real-World Test: Battery Performance

With a 5.0Ah battery, we achieved approximately 350 screws (100mm into softwood) before needing a recharge. In mixed use including some drilling, a single charge lasted a full 8-hour working day.

Who Should Buy This?

The DCF887 is ideal for:

  • Professional tradespeople: Build quality and performance justify the investment
  • Serious DIYers: If you do regular projects, this will last years
  • Deck builders: The power and precision are perfect for this application

Who shouldn't buy: If you only need an impact driver once or twice a year, there are cheaper options that would suffice. The DCF887 is overkill for occasional DIY use.
